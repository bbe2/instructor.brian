Comparison of Model Evaluation Methods

  • factors of comparison between different methods

Model Evaluation vs. Model Tuning

  • Model evaluation is not part of model tuning parameter
    • if you get a better performance through one evaluation method, it doesn't mean the model is better
    • don't change evaluation method parameters in order to get a better model (same underlying models)

Model performance metrics

  • Accuracy
  • Special challenges: imbalanced data
  • Confusion matrix derived metrics
    • TP, TN, FP, FN
    • Precision vs. recall
    • F measure
    • Sensitivity vs. specificity
    • Kappa

Confusion Matrix

Table Acutal Yes Actual No
Predicted Yes True Positive (TP) False Positive (FP)
Predicted No False Negative (FN) True Negative (TN)
  • Accuracy: percent of the predictions that are correct
    \[ Accuracy = \frac{TP + TN}{TP + FP + FN + TN} \]
  • Kappa (\(\kappa\)): measures inter-rater aggreement for qualitative (categorical) items. It is more robust by considering the possibility of the agreement occurring by chance.
    • \(\kappa = \frac{p_o-p_e}{1-p_e}\) \(p_o\): observed agreement among raters; \(p_e\): hypothetical probability of chance agreement.

Precision, Recall and F-measure (1)

  • Originates from information retrieval. Derived for each of possible class labels. such as Precision(Class = "Churn"), or Recall(Class = "Not Churn") or F(Class = "Churn")

  • Precision (positive predictive value): percent of predicted positive that are correct (in IR context, fraction of relevant instances among the retrieved instances) \[ Precision = \frac{TP}{TP + FP} \]

  • Recall (sensitivity): percent of positive cases that are correctly predicted (in IR context, fraction of the relevant documents that are successfully retrieved) \[ Recall = \frac{TP}{TP + FN} \]

Precision, Recall and F-measure (2)

  • F-measure (F1 or F score):
    • Balance between precision and recall (Harmonic Mean).
    • Range between 0 and 1 (perfect precision and recall) \[ F measure = \frac{2 * Precision * Recall}{Precision + Recall} \]

Sensitivity vs. Specificity

  • often used in medical dignostic testings or screening study which have positive vs. negative testing results
  • sensitivity (True Positive Rate or TPR, or recall): Percent of positive cases correctly predicted
  • Specificity (True Negative Rate or TNR): Percent of negative cases correctly predicted

Receiver Characteristic Curve (ROC)

  • ROC plots Sensitivity vs. (1 - Specificity) or TPR vs. FPR
  • Area Under Curve (AUC)
